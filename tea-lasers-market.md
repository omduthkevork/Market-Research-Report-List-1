<p><p>TEA (Transversely Excited Atmospheric) lasers are classified into three main types: Carbon Dioxide Lasers, Excimer Lasers, and Nitrogen Lasers. Carbon Dioxide Lasers are widely used in industrial cutting and medical applications, employing a gas mix to produce efficient infrared light. Excimer Lasers utilize reactive gases for precise ablation in processes like semiconductor manufacturing and eye surgery. Nitrogen Lasers operate using nitrogen gas to produce short, powerful pulses of ultraviolet light, often applied in various scientific research and material processing fields.</p></p>
