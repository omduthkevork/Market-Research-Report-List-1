<p><p>On-Demand Food Delivery Platforms operate primarily in two market types. The Restaurant-to-Consumer Delivery model allows restaurants to deliver meals directly to customers using their own staff or third-party drivers. In contrast, the Platform-to-Consumer Delivery model relies on intermediaries, where the delivery service partners with various restaurants to deliver food through their own drivers. Both models cater to consumer convenience, offering diverse dining options, but differ in the logistics and branding associated with food delivery.</p></p>
